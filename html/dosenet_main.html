Building a Community

DoseNet aims to form a network with schools throughout the Bay Area and beyond that will serve as to establish a relationship between UC Berkeley and member high schools. We see interacting with young students in this way as vital to improving community understanding of radiation and radiation dangers as well as providing valuable hands-on experimentation in a relevant and cutting edge scientific endeavor. To aid in building this community, we plan to have semi-yearly workshops at which involved students across the network can present the results from measurements taken at their location.

Student Development

Developing this radiation sensor network will help to illustrate naturally occurring background radiation and associated temporal and spatial variations. Research projects can be performed with the data collected through the network to more quantitatively describe the observations and uncertainties associated with them. Through Berkeley Radwatch and the DoseNet community will provide a forum for dialogue among experts, educators, students, and ultimately the public.

Several young undergraduates have already been directly involved in the construction and interfacing. There is the potential for interested advanced high school students to continue with this development to build new models for in-class use as well as upgrade them to include additional capabilities.

Going beyond Dosimeters

This network has the potential to continue expanding across California, the US and ultimately internationally. We foresee integrating our dosimeter network, along with any future capabilities such as air quality/temperature monitoring, with other such networks already in use around the US.
